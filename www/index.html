Benchmark Your Returns With Indexes

Investors look to broad indexes as benchmarks to help them gauge not only how well the markets are performing, but also how well they, as investors, are performing. For those who own stocks, they look to indexes like the S&P 500, the Dow Jones Industrial Average and the Nasdaq Composite to tell them ''where the market is''. The values of these indexes are displayed every day by financial media outlets all over the world. Most investors hope to meet or exceed the returns of these indexes over time.
To read more about indexes, see The ABCs Of Stock Indexes and Index Investing Tutorial.
